TinkerCAD simulation

Before testing out the circuit on the breadboard, I first used a electronic simulation website known as TinkerCAD to see wether my circuit works. Before creating the actual circuit on the website, I first calibrate my virtual LDR with the starter code provided by TinkerCAD. After that, I connected the circuit together and it works.

Breadboard testing

After trying out on TinkerCAD, I connected the circuit together on a breadboard.

I first get the LDR from fablab. Then, using the starter code found in TinkerCAD, I calibrated the LDR.

Then, I started to connect the actual circuit together. At first the circuit does not really work on a breadboard. I slowly figure out that the toggle switch used is not meant for breadboard. So, I decided to connect a jumper wire directly from Vcc or GND depending on wether i want it to be 'on' or 'off'. This time, the circuit works perfectly.

Actual stripboard connection

After trying out the circuit on the breadboard, I decided to solder the circuit onto the stripboard. As my stripboard has no layout, I have to solder jumper wires to every node of the circuit. However, I still prefer to use a no-layout stripboard. This is because I do not need to break the layout of the stripboard. Although this is my second time soldering in my whole life and my first time soldering for a graded assignment, I am able to get the circuit working on my first attempt abliet with some desoldering here and there.
